About

Born of iron-red hills,
shaped by the Gita

I grew up in the small town of Dalli Rajhara in Chhattisgarh — known for its iron ore mines, its mango groves, and the quiet tempo of life beneath old temple bells. It is the kind of place where evening aarti drifts across still air, and where roots grow deep into the red earth.

I now call Durg home. I build software — clean, honest systems — and I completed a B.Tech–M.Tech dual degree from the Biological Sciences and Bioengineering department at IIT Kanpur, where I also published research on metal-binding motifs in proteins.

Beyond code, I am drawn to the deeper questions — Hindu philosophy, classical literature, history, culture. To me, family, nature, health, and cultural continuity are the foundational pillars of a life well-lived. Technology, in this view, is never the destination — only a disciplined means toward dharma.

Work

Craft in the full-stack,
curiosity in the lab

I build software at Hardskills — products at the intersection of learning and human capability, built on React and Django. I care about systems that are purposeful and leave the user more capable than before they arrived.

My academic journey began in biology — the precise world of protein structures and biochemical logic — before finding its second form in engineering. The paper I published at IIT Kanpur on metal-binding motifs in proteins taught me something the Gita had already said: that attention, sustained and undivided, is itself a form of yoga.

I am also keenly aware of the psychological and societal weight technology carries. A tool that does not serve the full human — mind, body, culture — is a tool not yet complete.

Beyond Code

The life
between the lines

I am an avid trekker with over ten expeditions in the Himalayas. There is a particular quality to the silence above the treeline — the same quality I find in deep study, in a well-played chess game, or in the rhythm of a long run. It is the silence of full presence.

I read widely — history, economics, the Upanishads, the Mahabharata, classical literature. I believe the Hindu philosophical tradition offers frameworks of extraordinary depth for understanding consciousness, society, and the nature of right action in the world.

I also play football, cook, and write — always searching for the place where precision and joy coincide.
